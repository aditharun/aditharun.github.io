Adith Arun's Personal Site

Welcome! I am currently a junior at Johns Hopkins studying applied math. The main things that pique my interest revolve around medicine, healthcare, and technology. Specifically, I think that math combined with the right computational elements can power innovation when we ask the right questions.

I grew up in a small suburb near Sacramento, California where I spent a lot of time playing sports (competitive soccer, and basketball). I enjoy walking around and exploring new places. My favorite non-social things to do include reading, and writing code to figure out things like the best Catan strategy. In the spirit of sharing, my bookshelf is here. I am always looking to learn more, so feel free to reach out with books, or things you find interesting!

Here are a few interesting side projects:

  • Because of COVID-19 pushing most (tech) jobs remote, I got pretty interested in figuring out where a person should live to maximize their spending power. Using, gross tax data, I put together a site where you can enter an income and see where it ranks in a given city, county, or state. The code and details can be found here. There are cool things to build on top of this (i.e. comparing income across different demographics like age and ethnicity) that are awaiting development.
  • To sort through GTEX data and compare genes of interest across tissues, I hacked together an interactive tool that shows the expression of genes across many tissue types and their relative prevalence in a given tissue. There are a lot of avenues for further development & improvememt...this was mostly to teach myself to use Shiny in R.
