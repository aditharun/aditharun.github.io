I am currently an M2 at the Yale School of Medicine. I work at Delfi Diagnostics to develop new approaches for detecting cancer early and monitoring response to treatment using cell-free DNA fragmentation patterns. I also work with Harlan Krumholz to understand and investigate health disparities using outcomes data. I am interested in digital health, precision medicine, and building technology-driven solutions to deliver better healthcare experiences.

Previously, I received a BS in applied math and statistics at Johns Hopkins focusing on statistical learning. At JHU, I worked with Victor Velculescu and Rob Scharpf on methods for early cancer detection using liquid biopsies and understanding the biological origins of cell-free DNA. I also spent time with Gustavo Stolovitzky at IBM Research developing ensemble learning algorithms and modeling combination cancer therapy.

I am available for select consulting projects. I draw on my experiences and expertise in statistics, machine learning, computational biology, and diagnostics and therapeutics. Services I provide include due diligence evaluating the market and technology of a potential investment, designing (or reviewing designs) of statistics and ML methods for problems in medicine or biology, background education of statistics and science to technical and non-technical teams (see posts), and writing code (primarily in R) to analyze data and make intuitive and clean visualizations.
