  • Because of COVID-19 pushing most tech jobs remote, I got pretty interested in figurnig out where a person should live to maximize their spending power. I found articles like this that say what the top 1% salary is for a given state and a study by EPI looking at income distributions. I wanted to make a tool that people could use to see where there particular income stands in a given place. Check out where your income ranks in a given city, county, or state here! This uses gross SOI tax data from the most recent year available, constructs a MLE for an exponential distribution that best represents the data and shows your income quantile (i.e. your income ranks in the top x% of a given locale). The code can be found here. As a couple of my friends pointed out, this could be extended by considering race, gender, age, etc. where you can now compare your income across many different demographics. Plans for development have been made... I just haven't got around to coding it yet.
  • To help sort through the massive amount of GTEX data and easily compare genes of interest across tissues (a problem I ran into), I quickly hacked together an interactive tool (built using R Shiny) that shows the expression of genes across many tissue types and their relative prevalence in a given tissue. There are a lot of avenues for further development...
