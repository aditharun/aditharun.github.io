I am currently an MD Candidate at the Yale School of Medicine. I work at Delfi Diagnostics to develop new approaches for detecting cancer early using cell-free DNA fragmentation patterns. I am interested in digital health, precision medicine, and methods for early detection and monitoring of disease.

Previously, I received a BS in applied math and statistics at Johns Hopkins focusing on statistical learning. At JHU, I worked with Dr. Victor Velculescu and Dr. Rob Scharpf on methods for early cancer detection using liquid biopsies and understanding the biological origins of cell-free DNA. I also spent time with Dr. Gustavo Stolovitzky at IBM Research developing ensemble learning algorithms and modeling combination cancer therapy.
