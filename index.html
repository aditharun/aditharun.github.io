About

I am currently in medical school at Yale, where I just finished my third year. I work on health outcomes research (with Harlan Krumholz) and cancer genomics.

I am interested in digital health, precision medicine, and leveraging AI to solve problems in medicine and healthcare. I like thinking critically about medical research, clinical trials, biotech companies, and statistics. I write about these things on my Substack.

Previously, I worked at Delfi Diagnostics as a data scientist to develop new approaches for detecting cancer early and monitoring response to treatment using cell-free DNA fragmentation patterns.

From 2018 to 2021, I was an undergraduate at Johns Hopkins where I majored in applied math and studied the origins of cell-free DNA and genomic features for cancer early detection in the Velculescu Lab. I spent my summers during this time at IBM Research working to develop new ensemble learning algorithms and models for identifying synergistic drug combinations.
